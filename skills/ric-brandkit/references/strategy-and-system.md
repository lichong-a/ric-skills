# Brand Strategy And System

Use this reference when defining identity concepts and evaluating whether they form a coherent system.

## Strategy Inputs

- Audience and category.
- Brand promise and desired perception.
- Differentiation from relevant competitors.
- Cultural and language context.
- Required digital, physical, and environmental applications.
- Existing equity that must be preserved.

## Concept Methods

Potential concept methods include:

- Symbol derived from a product action.
- Metaphor fusion grounded in the brand promise.
- Meaningful monogram.
- Negative-space relationship.
- Construction geometry tied to a real idea.
- Typographic or verbal identity.

Choose the method because it serves the strategy, not because it is visually fashionable.

## System Components

Define the role of:

- Primary and secondary marks.
- Wordmark and typography.
- Color and accessibility behavior.
- Image and illustration language.
- Icons, patterns, and motion.
- Layout and composition.
- Voice and copy tone.

The components should express one idea while remaining flexible across required applications.

## Anti-Generic Review

Reject or challenge:

- Arbitrary initials without meaning.
- Common symbols that are indistinguishable in the category.
- Presentation effects hiding a weak mark.
- Random mockups unrelated to actual usage.
- Palette and type choices with no strategic reason.
- Identity systems that only work in one dark presentation board.

## Production Cautions

- Generated wordmarks and lettering require manual reconstruction and verification.
- Similarity and trademark checks require appropriate legal or search processes.
- Accessibility, localization, print, embroidery, and small-size constraints may require variants.
- Concept imagery is not automatically production-ready artwork.
